Eight Life Lessons from an Unlikely Entrepreneur

Choice

  • Learn to love the challenge
  • Keep focus on where you are going
  • Play the long game - it's a marathon not a sprint
  • The journey is never a straight line
Accept these things as true and it really changes the way you approach problems. Maybe share my story of how I approached term papers and large assignment deadlines. With the focus on the feeling after everything was completed. That day would come eventually.

Opportunity

  • Create them
  • Every problem is an opportunity

Action

  • What are you doing today that will get you closer to where you want to go?
  • Don't confuse activity with progress
  • "The only way out is through"

Knowledge

  • Be intentional and put yourself in situtations to learn
  • No one can take this from you (nor is anyone going to give it to you)
  • How do you become an expert? ... Make enough of mistakes
  • Aim for in-depth knowledge across multiple disciplines
I always took on new projects and worked in something new that I hadn't done before. Getting paid to learn. Start with the T shape, but end up looking for like a comb.

Wealth

  • Be financially literate. Learn about assets, liabilities, balance sheets
  • "It takes money to make money" - money vs time
  • Go turn $5 into $100
Prolly dont even need the initial $5 - just go browse the free listings on craigslist. It's really just trading time for $$ when you don't have the $$. Until you have the $$.

Brand

  • Don't underestimate the power in word-of-mouth
  • 'Personal Brand' I've seen continue to grow in popularity when it comes to recruitment
  • wolfymaster is who I am and will probably forever be

Community

  • You are a reflection of the 3-5 people you hang around . . choose wisely
  • Misery loves company
  • Soft skills FTW

Persistence

  • It's easy to give up on something when it doesn't feel like you have much invested in it
  • Haters gonna hate
  • If at first second third forth you don't succeed, try, try again
